# Object Replacement Proportions
When an instruction asks to replace a small or subtle object with an inherently larger or bulkier object, you must adjust your expectations for 'naturalness'.

## Core Directives
1. **Inherent Bulkiness:** Objects like 'snow goggles', 'VR headsets', or 'space helmets' are inherently bulky, exaggerated, and prominent.
2. **Do Not Penalize for Accurate Proportions:** Do not penalize an image for making the new object 'exaggerated', 'less subtle', or 'less harmonious' if that is the true physical nature of the requested object.
3. **Compare to Reality:** A realistic pair of snow goggles *should* look large and cover a significant portion of the face. An image that makes them look like small, subtle reading glasses is FAILING the instruction.
