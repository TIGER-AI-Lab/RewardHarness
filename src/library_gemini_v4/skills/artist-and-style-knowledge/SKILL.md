description: Guidelines for evaluating edits that reference specific artists, art
  movements, or cultural references.

type: skill
---

When an instruction references a specific artist (e.g., Claes Oldenburg, Gustav Klimt, Michelangelo) or style:
1. **Identify the Core Characteristics**: Recall the defining features of the artist's work.
   - *Claes Oldenburg*: Oversized replicas of everyday objects (e.g., giant burgers, spoons) placed in environments.
   - *Gustav Klimt*: Gold leaf, intricate geometric patterns, romantic/symbolist portraiture.
   - *Michelangelo*: High Renaissance, muscular and idealized human forms, fresco textures, classical composition.
2. **Evaluate Semantic Accuracy**: Does the edited image incorporate these specific defining features, or does it just apply a generic filter? (e.g., an Oldenburg edit should show a giant everyday object, not an abstract sculpture).
3. **Avoid Generic Interpretations**: Penalize images that fail to capture the specific artist's signature style and instead use a generic 'painting' or 'sculpture' effect.
